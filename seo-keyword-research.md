## 10. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE SUMMARY

### Tier 1 Competitors (massive domain authority, impossible to outrank on head terms)
- **GOV.UK** - Dominates all government/process queries
- **Citizens Advice** - Dominates legal rights queries
- **Age UK** - Strong on practical "what to do" content
- **Marie Curie** - Strong on emotional + practical
- **MoneyHelper** - Strong on financial queries

### Tier 2 Competitors (strong authority, difficult but not impossible)
- **Cruse Bereavement** - Dominates emotional/grief queries
- **The Gazette** - Strong on probate/executor queries
- **Which? Legal** - Strong on consumer-focused bereavement
- **Co-op Legal Services** - Strong on probate and executor content

### Tier 3 Competitors (beatable, similar domain authority)
- **Farra (withfarra.co.uk)** - Comprehensive guides, well-optimised. Main direct competitor.
- **Life Ledger (lifeledger.com)** - Individual company notification pages. Monetises via paid service.
- **Settld (settld.care)** - Similar to Life Ledger. Company-specific notification pages.
- **digitalgoodbye.co.uk** - Good on digital/subscription cancellation queries.
- **Bereavement Advice Centre (bereavementadvice.org)** - Older site, decent authority but dated content.
- **AfterLoss.uk** - Direct name competitor! Has funeral costs page, executor guide. Watch carefully.
- **Legacy Trail (legacytrail.co.uk)** - Company-specific notification guides.
- **untanglegrief.com** - Bank notification guides.

### Where HelpAfterLoss Can Win
1. **Bank and utility-specific pages** - Life Ledger and Settld do this but their pages are thin (designed to funnel users to their paid service). HelpAfterLoss can create genuinely comprehensive free guides.
2. **Long-tail financial queries** - "mortgage after death", "debts after death", "council tax after death" have fragmented competition (mostly solicitor blogs and local councils).
3. **Personalisation** - No competitor offers the smart questionnaire + personalised checklist approach.
4. **FAQ/featured snippet targeting** - Most competitors don't optimise for featured snippets. A well-structured FAQ page could capture many.
5. **Freshness** - Most charity sites have dated content. HelpAfterLoss can keep figures and processes current for 2026.
6. **All-in-one resource** - Most sites cover one aspect (emotional OR practical OR financial). HelpAfterLoss covers everything.
